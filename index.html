Why Hoof Care Matters

"No hoof, no horse" is the oldest truth in horsemanship. Proper farrier care — on the right schedule, with the right tools, tailored to your horse's breed and diet — is the foundation of soundness, performance, and a long working life. This site is your free, no-fluff guide to getting it right.

Quick Tips
📅
Trim Every 6–8 Weeks
Most horses need farrier attention every 6 to 8 weeks, whether shod or barefoot. Longer intervals cause imbalance and long-term joint stress.
🧹
Pick Hooves Daily
Daily hoof picking removes packed dirt, manure, and debris that cause thrush, white line disease, and abscesses. Two minutes a day prevents weeks of lameness.
💧
Watch Moisture Balance
Hooves that cycle between wet and dry crack and chip. Consistent footing, proper drainage, and hoof conditioner applied to the coronary band make a real difference.
🌿
Biotin Takes Time
Hoof supplements like biotin show results in 9–12 months — the time it takes a full hoof to grow out. Start early, be consistent, and don't expect overnight miracles.
